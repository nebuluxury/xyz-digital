Ideas to find them

You already know more prospects than you think

You don't have to be a salesperson. Here are easy, no-pressure ways to spot referrals in everyday life.

1

Start with people you know

Every friend or family member who owns a business, or works at one, is a warm intro. Just ask: "Who do you know with an old website?"

2

Drive down Main Street

The shops you pass every day - the auto shop, the cleaner, the salon. Pull up their site on your phone. Half of them will be outdated.

3

Google "[service] near me"

Search any local service and click the results. The ugly, slow, or missing sites are your list. Screenshot and send them our way.

4

Places you already spend money

Your dentist, mechanic, groomer, gym. You're already a customer - mentioning it feels natural, not salesy.

5

Local Facebook groups

Post one of our ready-made graphics (below) offering a free redesign. Let interested owners come to you in the comments.

6

Ask, then hand off

All you say is: "I know a team building great sites for $500 - want a free mockup, no obligation?" If yes, send us the name. We take it from there.
